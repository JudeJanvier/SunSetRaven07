1. What Army Maintenance Is

Army maintenance is a commander’s program focused on keeping all equipment Fully Mission Capable (FMC) using:

  • Preventive Maintenance Checks and Services (PMCS)
  • Scheduled services
  • Repairs and troubleshooting
  • Parts ordering and tracking
  • Reporting through GCSS-Army
  • AOAP, dispatching, licensing, safety, and records management

The goal is maximizing readiness, preventing failures, and ensuring equipment is available to execute the mission.

2. Day-to-Day Operations

Motor Pool Monday Battle Rhythm

  • 0630–0700: Formation, safety brief, work assignments
  • 0700–0900: Operators conduct PMCS on all assigned equipment
  • 0900–1000: Mechanics verify faults, apply diagnostics, and update GCSS‑Army
  • 1000–1200: Submit parts requests (ZPARK), order lubes, initiate work orders
  • 1300–1600: Corrective maintenance, services, backlog repair, recovery ops
  • End of Day: Update trackers, report status to maintenance control and commander

Services and Preventive Maintenance

Operators use the -10 series TM to check fluids, inspect safety items, identify issues, and record faults on DA 5988‑E. Scheduled services are conducted by 91-series mechanics at set intervals.

Common Motor Pool Trackers

  • Service Tracker: Lists all equipment, service due dates, and status.
  • NMC Tracker / Deadline Report: Shows all down equipment and reason codes.
  • Parts Tracker: Everything on order, status, and document numbers.
  • AOAP Tracker: Samples due, submitted, and overdue.
  • Dispatch Log: All vehicles out for mission.
  • Work Order Tracker: DS/GS-level support status.

Parts Tracking & AOAP

The PLL (92A or 92Y) manages parts from identification and request through GCSS-A to installation. The Army Oil Analysis Program (AOAP) prevents catastrophic failure through routine oil sampling and analysis, reducing unplanned failures and costs.

3. MOS Breakdown – Motor Pool Organizational Structure

91 Series (Primary Mechanics – The Backbone)

  • 91B – Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic: Repairs HMMWVs, LMTVs, JLTVs, etc.
  • 91H – Track Vehicle Repairer: Repairs tracked vehicles like Bradleys.
  • 91D – Power Generation Equipment Repairer: Repairs generators, A/C units.
  • 91E – Allied Trades Specialist: Fabricates and welds parts.
  • 91L – Construction Equipment Repairer: Repairs engineer equipment.

92 Series (Supply & PLL)

  • 92A – Automated Logistical Specialist: Runs PLL, orders parts, manages stock.
  • 92Y – Unit Supply: Manages TMDE, dispatch books, tool rooms.

88 Series (Transportation Support)

  • 88M – Motor Transport Operator: Drivers who perform PMCS and report faults.

Leadership & Command Roles

  • Motor Sergeant / Maint. Control NCOIC: Oversees all maintenance ops.
  • Commander / XO / 1SG: Responsible for overall readiness, resources, and personnel.
